About

I am a Master's student in the Department of Political Science at the University of British Columbia. My research interests focus on quantitative methods in political science, with particular attention to political behavior and the political economy of development, especially in the Chinese context.

My current research examines Chinese public opinion about US-China relations, where I serve as a Graduate Research Assistant for Professor Xiaojun Li. I am particularly interested in applying statistical methods to understand the formation of political attitudes and beliefs, with a focus on the political economy of development in China.

Prior to graduate school, I completed my MA (SocSci) in Politics with Quantitative Methods (undergraduate degree) at the University of Glasgow, graduating with First Class Honours in 2024.

Research Interests

  • Formation of Political Attitudes and Beliefs
  • Party Politics and Political Behavior
  • Political Economy of Development (China)
  • Experimental and Quantitative Methods
  • Panel Data and Causal Inference
  • Survey Experiments and Bayesian Methods
